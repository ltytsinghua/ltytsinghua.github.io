We revisit the long-standing question of whether the residual entropy of hexagonal ice
equals that of cubic ice using high-precision tensor-network methods. Encoding the ice
rule explicitly into local tensors, the residual entropy is transformed into finding the
largest eigenvalue of a transfer operator built from a projected entangled-pair operator.
Combined with our split corner-transfer matrix renormalization group algorithm, this
allows a direct high-accuracy computation of both residual entropies and provides
numerical evidence supporting their equality.
